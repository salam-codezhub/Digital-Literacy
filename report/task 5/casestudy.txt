Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is a common type of cybercrime in India where fraudsters trick users into sending money through digital payment apps.
It usually happens when a scammer contacts a victim via phone call, message, or social media pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive.
The fraudster sends a fake payment request or a malicious link and convinces the victim to approve it by saying it is required to receive money or complete a transaction. 
In reality, instead of receiving money, the victim ends up sending money to the scammer. Sometimes, fraudsters also ask for OTPs or PINs, which should never be shared.
Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. 
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes identity theft if personal details are leaked.
This type of fraud is increasing due to the growing use of digital payments. Awareness and caution are essential to avoid becoming a victim.
